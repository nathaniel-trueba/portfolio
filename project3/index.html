Project 3: Exploring Global Temperature Trends

An Interactive Climate Visualization

Group members: Nidhi Manayilakath (nmanayilakath@ucsd.edu), Nathaniel Trueba (ntrueba@ucsd.edu), Tijil Chhabra (tchhabra@ucsd.edu)

Overview

How will Earth's average surface temperature evolve over the next century and how much does the outcome depend on emissions today? This project combines observed historical temperature data from NASA GISTEMP with CMIP6 multi-model projections from the IPCC Sixth Assessment Report to explore three Shared Socioeconomic Pathway scenarios. All temperature anomalies are reported relative to the 1850–1900 pre-industrial baseline. Data processing was performed in a companion Jupyter notebook; the visualizations load the resulting CSVs directly.

Visualization 1 — Temperature Anomaly Over Time

Global mean surface temperature anomaly (°C above pre-industrial baseline) from 1880 to 2100. Historical observations come from NASA GISTEMP v4, rebased to 1850–1900 by adding +0.69°C (IPCC AR6 Cross-Chapter Box 1.2). Projection bands show the IPCC AR6 assessed likely range for each scenario. Toggle scenarios on/off; hover for exact values.

Visualization 2 — Regional Warming by Country

Projected surface temperature anomaly (°C above pre-industrial baseline) by country, from 2024 to 2100. Values are derived from IPCC AR6 WGI Atlas assessed regional warming medians for 46 macro-regions; each country is assigned to its AR6 reference region. Drag the year slider or change the scenario to update the map. Hover for country-level details.

Visualization 3 — Scenario Comparison

Projected global mean temperature anomaly (°C above pre-industrial) for each SSP scenario at a selected year. Drag the slider to watch the gap between scenarios widen over time — the difference between aggressive mitigation and high emissions grows from negligible in the near term to nearly 3°C by 2100.

Visualization 4 — Warming Stripes

Each stripe represents one year of global mean temperature anomaly from 1880 to present, colored from deep blue (cooler) to deep red (warmer) relative to the 1850–1900 pre-industrial baseline. The shift from blue to red across the chart makes the long-run warming trend immediately visible. Click any stripe for the exact value.

Visualization 5 — Country Warming Race

The 15 countries projected to warm the most at a selected year and scenario, ranked by anomaly. Drag the slider to watch countries rise and fall in the ranking as warming accelerates differently across regions. High-latitude countries dominate at every time horizon due to polar amplification.

Visualization 6 — Temperature vs Precipitation Change

Global mean precipitation change (% vs 1850–1900 baseline) plotted against temperature anomaly for each SSP scenario at a selected year. Derived from CMIP6 ensemble medians (ACCESS-CM2, CanESM5, MPI-ESM1-2-LR). As warming increases, the global water cycle intensifies — wetter conditions globally, though regional patterns vary. Drag the slider to watch both variables evolve together.
